**Name**: Mini Jack to Alligator Clips Cable

**Type**: Connector

**Principle**: Passive adapter cable with TRS mini jack on one end and three alligator clips on the other for quick temporary connections.

**Function**: Connects Organolib mini jack cables to bare wires, component leads, or other surfaces via spring-loaded alligator clips. Enables quick prototyping without soldering.

**Voltage**: 0-12V (low voltage/current only)

**Typical use**: Quick prototyping connections, testing components, temporary wiring, connecting to exposed terminals, educational demonstrations, breadboard alternatives.

**Good for**:

- Rapid prototyping without soldering
- Temporary test connections
- Connecting to exposed wires/terminals
- Quick circuit modifications
- Educational demonstrations
- Field testing

**Bad for**:

- Permanent installations
- High current (>1A)
- High voltage (>12V)
- Reliable long-term connections
- Vibration-prone environments
- Small/delicate component leads

**Mini Jack connection diagram**:

TRS Mini Jack:

- **Tip** → Red alligator clip (signal/voltage)
- **Ring** → Grey/other color alligator clip (signal/IO)
- **Sleeve** → Black alligator clip (ground)

**Make instructions**:

Can be made by:

1. Get TRS mini jack plug
2. Solder three wires to tip/ring/sleeve
3. Attach alligator clips to wire ends
4. Use heat shrink for strain relief

**Use instructions/Tutorials**:

1. Plug mini jack into Organolib element
2. Clip alligators to target connections (wires, terminals, component leads)
3. Observe polarity: Black to ground, Red to signal/voltage, Grey to IO
4. Ensure secure clip contact (check for looseness)
5. Avoid short circuits between clips
