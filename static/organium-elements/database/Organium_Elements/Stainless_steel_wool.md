**Name**: Stainless Steel Wool (Conductive Fiber)

**Type**: Material

**Principle**: Electrically conductive fine metal fibers. Stainless steel fibers (~20µm diameter) provide electrical conductivity and can be integrated into textile and composite materials.

**Function**: Conductive material component used in making conductive textiles, felted materials, and wearable electronics. Can be blended with other fibers for electrical conductivity.

**Voltage**: N/A (passive conductive material, voltage depends on application)

**Typical use**: Conductive felted wool creation, wearable electronics, soft sensors, textile interfaces, conductive thread alternative, pressure-sensitive fabrics, capacitive touch surfaces.

**Good for**:

- Creating conductive textiles
- Blending with wool for felting
- Soft, flexible conductivity
- Wearable electronics
- Textile sensors
- EMI shielding in flexible form

**Bad for**:

- High-current applications
- Precision electrical connections
- Rigid structures
- Long-term outdoor use (corrosion)
