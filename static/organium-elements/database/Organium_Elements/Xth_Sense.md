**Name**: Xth Sense (Biophysical Music Augmentation)

**Type**: Sensor

**Principle**: Biophysical sensing. Amplifies subtle sounds produced by muscle contractions (mechanomyography). Captures acoustic vibrations from muscle fibers through contact microphone and specialized amplification.

**Function**: Wearable sensor strapped to limbs that captures and amplifies sounds of muscles working. Converts internal muscle sounds into audible audio signals for listening or control parameter mapping.

**Voltage**: 9V battery power (typical for biometric sensors requiring amplification)

**Typical use**: Biophysical music performance, muscle-sound mapping to synthesis parameters, gestural control, body-based instruments, experimental performance interfaces, augmented body interaction.

**Good for**:

- Detecting limb motion through muscle sound
- Mapping physical gesture to audio/control parameters
- Unique biophysical sound generation
- Intimate body-sound relationship

**Mini Jack connection diagram**:

**Component diagram**: Contact sensor (strapped to limb) → Specialized amplifier → Audio output

**Extra and gratuitous information and images**:

**Creator**: Developed by Marco Donnarumma as biophysical music augmentation system.

**Sensing principle**: Muscles produce acoustic vibrations during contraction. Xth Sense amplifies these normally inaudible sounds into audible/controllable signals.

**Applications**:

- Biophysical music performance
- Gestural sound control
- Body-based instrument augmentation
- Experimental performance art
- Muscle-sound composition

**Placement**: Best results on large muscle groups (biceps, forearms, calves, thighs). Different muscles produce different sonic characteristics.

**Signal processing**: Raw muscle sounds can be used directly or processed through effects, amplitude tracking, spectral analysis, or mapped to synthesis parameters.
