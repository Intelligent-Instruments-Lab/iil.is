**Name**: Velcro Prototyping Panel

**Type**: Material, Mounting System

**Principle**: Hook-and-loop fastener (Velcro) mounting system on rigid panel. Allows quick attachment/detachment and repositioning of components during prototyping.

**Function**: Provides easily reconfigurable mounting surface for components during prototyping. Components with Velcro backing can be quickly attached, removed, and rearranged without tools.

**Voltage**: N/A (passive mounting hardware)

**Typical use**: Rapid prototyping, experimental setups, testing component layouts, reconfigurable installations, temporary instrument configurations, workshop demonstrations.

**Good for**:

- Quick component rearrangement
- Lightweight components
- Rapid prototyping workflows
- Temporary setups
- Testing layouts before permanent mounting
- Tool-free reconfiguration
- Educational demonstrations

**Make instructions**:

1. Cut plywood panel to desired size
2. Apply self-adhesive Velcro strips in grid pattern
3. Attach corresponding Velcro to component backs
4. Mount panel to wall or stand as needed

**Extra and gratuitous information and images**:

**Inspiration**: International Space Station (ISS) uses similar Velcro mounting systems for tool organization in zero gravity.

**Weight capacity**: Depends on Velcro quality and surface area. Typical self-adhesive Velcro holds ~100-500g per 10cm² strip. Heavier components require larger contact area.

**Panel construction**: Rigid backing (plywood) prevents flexing that would compromise Velcro grip. Can be mounted on walls, easels, or workbenches.

**Applications**: Component layout testing, modular instrument prototypes, reconfigurable installations, educational workshops, temporary setups
